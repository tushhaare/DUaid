DU plans for Fourth Year under UGCF-2022



Delhi University’s Four-Year Undergraduate Program: What Students Need to Know

Delhi University is gearing up for the fourth year under the Undergraduate Curriculum Framework 2022 (UGCF-2022). For the first time, students will undertake a four-year undergraduate course under the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020.

Extended Class Hours to Manage Increased Student Intake

To accommodate the additional batch of students, DU and its colleges are extending class hours until late evening. Officials state that this will help manage resources within existing infrastructure. However, concerns regarding faculty availability, infrastructure limitations, and student interest remain points of discussion.

New Specialization Tracks in the Fourth Year

Under NEP 2020, students in their fourth year can choose from three specialization tracks:

  • Dissertation Writing – Designed for students interested in academic research.
  • Academic Projects – Focuses on applied research.
  • Entrepreneurship – Guides students in market research, business development, and financial planning.

Degree Structure Under NEP 2020

Students will receive different qualifications based on their years of study:

  • 1 Year – Certificate
  • 2 Years – Diploma
  • 3 Years – Bachelor's (Honours in one core subject) or Bachelor's (Multidisciplinary)
  • 4 Years – Bachelor's (Honours with Research) or Bachelor's (Honours in Multidisciplinary Courses)

Mandatory Research Methodology for Research Track

Students opting for Honours with Research must take Research Methodology as a General Elective (GE) in Semester VI or VII. However, those choosing Academic Projects or Entrepreneurship do not require this course.

Expanded Generic Elective (GE) Course Selection

The university has broadened the scope of Generic Electives (GEs). From Semester V, BA (Programme) students can choose GEs from an open pool, allowing them to explore disciplines beyond their core subjects.

Special Provisions for Hindi Language Exemption

For students who did not study Hindi up to Class 8, DU has introduced flexible options:

  • BA (Programme) and BCom (Programme) students with two non-language core disciplines can opt for a GE from the open pool if a GE (L) in their mother tongue is unavailable.
  • Students from South India and the Northeast, who lack Hindi proficiency, may receive exemptions from the GE (L) Hindi requirement.
  • Those who haven’t studied Hindi or Modern Indian Language up to Class 8 may also be exempted from the second language requirement.

Infrastructure and Scheduling Adjustments

To manage the increased academic load, DU plans to implement an 8 AM to 8 PM schedule as per UGC guidelines:

  • Lab timings, which currently extend only till 4 PM, will be revised.
  • Students will have mixed timetables, including evening classes on some days to ensure all academic credits are covered.

Challenges and Concerns Raised by College Principals

Ajay Arora, Principal, Ramjas College:

  • Students do not need a CGPA above 7.5 to enter the fourth year; the promotion criteria remain unchanged.
  • The number of students opting for the fourth year is uncertain, but any eligible student can enroll.
  • College infrastructure, originally designed for a three-year program, requires adjustments for classroom and resource expansion.
  • Timetable changes will lead to reduced flexibility in student schedules.

Conclusion

While DU’s implementation of the four-year undergraduate course under UGCF-2022 offers flexibility and research opportunities, concerns around infrastructure, faculty availability, and student interest remain unresolved. As DU transitions into this new academic structure, students and stakeholders must stay updated on the latest developments.
